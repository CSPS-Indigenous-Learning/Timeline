Definition of a Job Description

A job description is a document that describes the work requirements of a position. It contains all the information which, according to the job evaluation standard and the Directive on Classification, is needed to evaluate the work.

  • Sets out the requirements of a position in terms of skills, responsibilities, effort, and working conditions, in accordance with the Canadian Human Rights Act.
  • Integrates the requirements of an organization with respect to the knowledge and skills necessary to support the objectives for products and service delivery.
  • Can be used to identify
    • The occupational group, level and salary range associated with a position, the determination of the bargaining agent, and the corresponding collective agreement.
    • The duties and competencies required for the position, which form the basis for staffing, training, decisions on official language requirements, and performance evaluations.
  • Must be brief and concise.

Did you know?

Managers must ensure that a job description accurately reflects the duties assigned to a position, and are responsible for updating it as required.
